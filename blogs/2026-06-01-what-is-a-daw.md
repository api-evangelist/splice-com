A DAW (digital audio workstation) is software that enables musicians to record, edit, and export audio files, making music production accessible without expensive physical equipment. The article traces DAWs' origins to 1980s German developers and discusses how to choose one based on budget and creative goals. Nearly every modern record uses at least one DAW, with popular options ranging from free to premium software.
